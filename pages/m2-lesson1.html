Module 2 Lesson 1 IU HPC Systems and Account Setup

Quartz, Big Red 200, Research Desktop, and Getting Started

UITS Research Technologies maintains two supercomputers at IU. Quartz is a high-throughput cluster for general and AI workloads. Big Red 200 is IU's flagship Cray system built for massively parallel computation. Research Desktop provides an always-on interactive Linux desktop accessible from any browser. All three systems share the same underlying storage groups and connect through the IU Science DMZ for optimized data movement.

The slides below walk through each resource and the account setup process from start to finish.

 Flashcards

Quartz

IU's high-throughput cluster for general HPC and AI workloads, with CPU-only and GPU-accelerated partitions.

Big Red 200

IU's flagship HPE Cray EX supercomputer for massively parallel multi-node computation, using the Cray Slingshot interconnect.

Research Desktop (RED)

A virtual Linux desktop on HPC infrastructure, accessible from any browser or ThinLinc. Sessions persist after disconnect.

Cray Slingshot interconnect

Big Red 200's internal network. 200 Gbps per port, low latency, designed for tightly coupled parallel jobs.

IU Science DMZ

A dedicated high-speed network segment at IU for large research data transfers between HPC systems and external institutions.

RT Project

A UITS Research Technologies project account. All batch job submissions on Quartz and Big Red 200 must be charged to one.

Quartz vs. Big Red 200

Quartz suits most general workflows. Big Red 200 is for jobs requiring tight parallelism across many nodes simultaneously.

Quartz "hopper" partition

12 Dell XE8640 nodes, each with 4 NVIDIA H100 GPUs and NVMe local SSD. Primarily for AI workloads.

Quartz GPU types

"gpu" partition: NVIDIA V100. "hopper" partition: NVIDIA H100.

Big Red 200 GPU nodes

64 nodes, each with 4 NVIDIA A100 40 GB GPUs.

Default storage allocation on Quartz

100 GB Geode home directory and 800 GB Slate personal space, expandable to 1600 GB.

Why Slate requires an HPC account first

Slate is only provisioned for users with an active Quartz or Big Red 200 compute account.

Where to create IU HPC accounts

one.iu.edu ("View Accounts") or access.iu.edu/Accounts.

  Did You Know?

Big Red 200 uses a Dragonfly network topology for its Slingshot interconnect, which minimizes the number of hops between any two nodes and keeps latency low even across hundreds of nodes.
