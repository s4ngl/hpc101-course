Welcome to HPC 101

About This Course

Want to learn about High Performance Computing (HPC)? This course introduces the principles of HPC and helps you become an effective, efficient, and conscientious consumer of IU HPC compute cycles and storage resources.

It should take you around two and a half to three hours to watch all of the video content. There are also short knowledge checks and quizzes throughout the course, and most will take less than 15 minutes to complete.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

Understand HPC Fundamentals

Develop knowledge of what supercomputers are, how parallel computing works, and how HPC supports scientific research and AI.

Access and Use IU's HPC Systems

Learn how to access IU systems, connect with SSH, and run workloads on research computing resources.

Manage Storage and Research Data

Understand IU storage systems and data organization strategies for managing research data.

Learn HPC Best Practices

Learn how to efficiently use compute and storage resources, transfer data, archive files, and get support.

Course Recognition

Learners who complete all required quizzes and activities will earn an HPC 101 completion badge recognizing foundational competency in IU's research computing environment. Complete all four module badges to earn the HPC 101 Certified microcredential.

What You'll Do

HPC 101 is organized into four modules with multiple lessons each. Throughout the course, you will:

  •   Watch interactive lecture videos (all approximately 10–15 minutes each)
  •   Complete short knowledge checks and practice navigating HPC systems and workflows
  •    Apply HPC concepts to real-world research and computing scenarios
