How Do LLM's Work? 

How do large language models (LLMs) work? This is important to know because it will help you understand why proper prompting is so important. Large language models are essentially prediction machines. Think of them as the world's best auto complete. It is trying to predict or complete based on the prompt you give it. So different prompts equal different output.  

Let's think about IU's fight song. As you know, we have this opening line: "Indiana, our Indiana, Indiana, we're all for..." If we were to plug this into a large language model, it will try to predict what word is next. GenAI platforms have learned connections by analyzing vast amounts of human generated knowledge. This includes things like books, the Internet, and even academic sources. When you enter a prompt, the model evaluates countless possible responses by their statistical likelihood and gives you a response it deems highly related or likely based on its training data. This is part of why prompting is so important. You want to activate the right parts within its vast training data.  

In our example, the prompt leads the model to return "you," just like any good Hoosier would know. However, a large language model won't always give you the same answer to the same prompt. Sometimes there's a bit of randomness in there. This is part of what can make large language models creative, and there are ways both simple and advanced to ramp up the creativity of large language models.  

Remember, though, the model doesn't actually understand what's true. It's simply returning statistically likely or related answers based on its training data. Like people, it can be wrong. So be careful about trusting its output.  

One common misconception is to think of GenAI as a search engine. It is not that. GenAI is a prediction and creation machine. It will do what it thinks you are asking it to do. Sometimes well and sometimes not. Iteration and working with GenAI is key here. One analogy is to think of a search engine like a library allowing access to different types of content that's already out there. On the other hand, GenAI is like a skilled chef who can make an entirely new dish out of existing ingredients. But you can easily send the dish back as many times as you'd like, asking for small iterations or even something entirely new.  

One additional thing that makes GenAI so powerful is that it is a tool that can use other tools.
