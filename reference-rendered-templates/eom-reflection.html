Instructions

Throughout this module, you were asked to complete small tasks. Let's revisit these scenarios and reflect.

Choose ONE of the lesson activities (recapped below). You can either complete the task and answer the reflection questions below, or check out the Reflection Prompt Templates in Guided Prompts for more structured prompts to help you get the best results.

Option 1: Visual Inputs

Find something visual you need help with using the screenshot technique. This could involve screenshotting a software feature and asking AI how to use it, uploading outfit options and asking which works for your interview, or taking a picture of the inside of your fridge or pantry and asking for meal suggestions.

Upload your image, ask a specific question, then keep the conversation going by asking for alternatives, clarification, or next steps.

After your chat, reflect on your experience using the following questions:

  • When you uploaded your image, what was the AI particularly good at understanding? What did it struggle with? 
  • Did the AI ask for more information to better understand your request, or did it make assumptions?
  • How impactful was the text prompt you wrote in helping the AI interpret the visual context correctly?
Option 2: Text & Document Inputs

Copy-paste one long article on a topic you're interested in that you've been avoiding. Ask three follow-up questions to dig deeper, always remembering to double-check the answers and not completely trust AI.

After learning about your article, reflect on your experience using the following questions: 

  • What are the most significant differences between using an AI to understand an article versus reading the article yourself? 
  • Did you notice any "hallucinations" or plausible but incorrect information?
Option 3: Understanding AI Hallucinations

Test AI on something you're an expert on, such as your favorite game, hometown, or hobby. Start asking AI detailed questions, beginning simply and then getting increasingly specific.

Pay attention to three things: when AI starts making things up, how confident it sounds when fabricating information, and the types of details it invents.

After testing AI, reflect on your experience using the following questions:

  • When the AI started to invent information, what were the first signs that something was wrong?
  • Did you notice any specific patterns in the types of details it made up, such as inventing a person's name or a fictional event?
  • What was the most effective way you found to verify the AI's increasingly specific claims?
