Introduction 

Most people only scratch the surface of what GenAI is capable of. While many treat it like a simple search engine or a fact machine, GenAI works best as a thought partner that can iterate, create, and collaborate with you across multiple formats and tasks. This lesson will give you an introduction to some key features available in large language models, helping you understand how to interact effectively with GenAI to immediately boost your productivity. I promise this will be a hands-on lesson, so let's dive right in and get you set up.  

Getting Started with Gemini 

Go to gemini.google.com. IU has provided subscriptions to all students, staff, and faculty to Gemini, Google's large language model. Let's access this resource. If you're logged into a personal account, you may want to log out first. One safe approach is to start a temporary window on your new browser. Click on the upper right-hand button to sign in with your IU credentials.  

But before we get started, one thing to keep in mind is that when you use IU Gemini, IU doesn't use your chats to improve the models. Also, keep in mind that the Gemini can make mistakes, so always double-check it.  

Similarities Between Gemini & Other LLM's 

There are a couple of things I want to highlight here. The first is that this chat window screen is very similar to what you'll see with other large language models, for example, ChatGPT, Claude, and Grok. While each has their own strengths and weaknesses, for the most part, they work nearly identically. And so skills you learn in this course should easily transfer over to other platforms.  

Now let's talk about the right way to interact with and, even more importantly, how not to interact with GenAI. GenAI is not a fact machine. Always think of it as a thought partner, just like Crimson here. If I ask Crimson to help me be more productive, I probably wouldn't get the best advice. It'd be pretty vague and generic.  

[Crimson shakes their head] 

But if I told Crimson, "I'm a project manager, spending three hours daily in meetings, but falling behind on deliverables. I need strategies to protect focused work time while maintaining team collaboration and stakeholder communication," Crimson would be able to give much more helpful output. 

Effective Prompting Strategies

 This is called prompting, and we will cover so much more in how to craft effective prompts and each lesson of the course, including giving you a prompt framework in Lesson 2.

[Brian's request is shown typed into Gemini. The output is 5 strategies to do "Empower & Delegate well", expanding on the selected strategy.] 

So you can see it knew the context of my question and expanded out that section on empowering and delegating, that third point. The big thing I want you to see here in this example is that you can easily iterate on the GenAI's output, working with it to even change the format and type of what you see.  

Another thing to always keep in mind is that these are generative. You can use the same exact prompt as me and get different output. That's also what makes iterating so important. Remember that these platforms are called GenAI. They're generative. That means if you don't like a response, they can create a fresh one for you based on its training data.  

There are many ways to do this. You could simply type "try again," or you could hit this redo button at the bottom of the answer, or you could prompt it to go a different direction altogether. Please also note the Share and Copy buttons. If you hit "Share and Export," you can notice that you can export the material to Google Docs or even to a draft in Gmail. Or you can hit "Copy" to save it to your clipboard. Under "More," you get additional options, including double-checking the response, copying, listening, or reporting legal issues.  

Now, for a really powerful and underused feature: you can give Gemini lots of important context. One great way to do this is by uploading files.  

Uploading Files 

Hit the plus icon right here to upload documents.  

[Plus sign button is shown directly below the Gemini input text box, labeled "Add files"] 

You can upload multiple files and can have Gemini help you with everything from summarizing these files to use them to derive new insights and innovation. More on that later, too.  

We spent some time on features, but now let's talk about different types of models. You can think of these as the brains of the LLM. Gemini has two available models. If you click the dropdown arrow up here, you'll see the options Flash and Pro. Flash is similar to other standard models like ChatGPT 4o. But Pro is what's called a reasoning model. More similar to ChatGPT O series, like ChatGPT o3 and o4 and its iterations. These names are something else, right?  

It's called a reasoning model because it spends more time reasoning or thinking through a task. These are great for very complex and difficult tasks, but not as good for everyday ones.  

[A brain made up of circuits is shown while futuristic music plays] 

They also have usage limits, so you want to use them carefully. We'll discuss more how these reasoning models work and how to use them for difficult problems later in the course.  

Next, I want to show you how chat windows work. If you click on these three bars in the upper left, you'll see your chat history. This helps you revisit earlier prompts and continue those conversations where you left off. That said, I recommend starting a new chat window for each distinct topic or session. It's easy. You just have to click right here. Remember how we could ask GenAI to expand on its answers a bit ago? When we create a new window by just clicking this button, it helps ensure that your current prompt isn't influenced by past context, which can sometimes lead to confusing or unexpected results. Once again, I highly recommend you do this each time you're tackling a new problem. 

Creating Images 

GenAI can also create images. But remember that AI-generated images are tools to enhance human creativity, not replace critical thinking about accuracy and appropriateness. Being aware of some of these capabilities may be helpful for you in brainstorming, creativity, and quick prototyping of ideas. Let's say you're able to secure an internship with Indianapolis Motor Speedway, and they ask you to think about marketing materials. How could GenAI help you? One prompt you could enter is "create an infographic visual of the Indy 500. Make it in a photo-realistic style." It'll take just a second to generate the image.  

[Screen shows a photo-realistic racetrack with many racecars driving towards the finish line and large crowds in the stands.] 

What do you think? Keep in mind that GenAI can make mistakes, so you should always check everything. You can also always add some facts of your own that you wish it to have. Remember, the iteration and conversation with GenAI is key to success.  

So let's have some fun with one last image here. Just to showcase how you can also give context and image generation, and give it in different styles, let's use the prompt: "draw me a picture of a Hoosier AI agent assistant for a GenAI skills course. Make it in a cartoon style".
